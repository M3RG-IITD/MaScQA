We can start by finding the surface area in $m^2$:
\begin{align*}
50\text{ }cm^2 &= 50 \times 10^{-4}\text{ }m^2 \\
&= 0.005\text{ }m^2
\end{align*}

Next, we can convert the mass loss to $kg$ and the time period to years:
\begin{align*}
200\text{ }g &= 0.2\text{ }kg \\
10\text{ }years &= 10\text{ }year
\end{align*}

Now, we can use the formula for corrosion rate:
$$
\text{Corrosion rate} = \frac{\text{Mass loss}}{\text{Surface area} \times \text{Time period}}
$$
Substituting the values, we get:
\begin{align*}
\text{Corrosion rate} &= \frac{0.2}{0.005\times 10} \\
&= 4\text{ }kg.m^{-2}.year^{-1}
\end{align*}

Rounding off to the nearest integer, the answer is: 

- 4

Therefore, the corrosion rate is 4 $kg.m^{-2}.year^{-1}$ (rounded off to the nearest integer).
