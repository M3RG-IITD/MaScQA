Let the steady state creep rate at 890K be denoted by $\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{890}}$ and at 980K be $\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{980}}$. The creep rate follows the Arrhenius equation:

$$\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss}=A\exp{\left(\frac{-Q}{RT}\right)}$$

where $A$ is a material constant, $Q$ is the activation energy (J/mol), $R$ is the gas constant(8.314 J/mol K) and $T$ is the temperature (K).

Taking the ratio of the creep rate at 980K to the creep rate at 890K gives:

$$\frac{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{980}}}{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{890}}}= \frac{A\exp{\left(\frac{-Q}{R(980)}\right)}}{A\exp{\left(\frac{-Q}{R(890)}\right)}}=20$$

Taking the logarithm of both sides results in:

$$\ln{\left(\frac{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{980}}}{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{890}}}\right)} = \frac{Q}{R}\left(\frac{1}{890}-\frac{1}{980}\right)$$

Solving for $Q$ we get:

$$Q = \frac{R\ln{\left(\frac{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{980}}}{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{890}}}\right)}}{\left(\frac{1}{890}-\frac{1}{980}\right)}$$

Now, using the information given in the question, we can find the steady state creep rate at the temperature of interest, $x$K, as follows:

$$\frac{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{x}}}{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{890}}}=\frac{\exp{\left(\frac{-Q}{R(x)}\right)}}{\exp{\left(\frac{-Q}{R(890)}\right)}}=5$$

Taking the logarithm of both sides, we get:

$$\ln{\left(\frac{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{x}}}{\dot{\varepsilon}_{ss_{890}}}\right)}=\frac{Q}{R}\left(\frac{1}{890}-\frac{1}{x}\right)$$

Substituting the expression for $Q$ and solving for $x$, we get:

$$x = \boxed{974} ~K$$

Therefore, the temperature at which the steady state creep rate is 5 times that at 890K is approximately 974K.
