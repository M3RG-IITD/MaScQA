Using lever rule: 
For 561° C eutectic, 
amount of $\alpha$ phase = $\dfrac{(36.9-14.48)}{(36.9-14.48) + (100-14.48)}$ × 100 wt.% = 25.07 wt.%
amount of $Mg_2Sn$ = $\dfrac{(100-14.48)}{(36.9-14.48) + (100-14.48)}$ × 100 wt.% = 74.93 wt.%

For 203° C eutectic,
amount of $\beta-Sn$ phase = 100 wt.%
amount of $Mg_2Sn$ phase = 0 wt.%

At just below 561° C, using the lever rule, 
% of $\alpha$ phase = $\dfrac{(50-25.1)}{(74.9-25.1)+(50-25.1)}$ × 100 % $\approx$ 38%. 

Therefore, option (B) 38 is the correct answer.
