We can use the rule of mixtures to determine the elastic modulus of the composite material. The rule of mixtures states that the overall properties of a composite material can be calculated by considering the properties of the individual components and their volume fractions. 

In this case, we have a polymer reinforced with 40 vol.% glass fiber. Therefore, the elastic modulus of the composite material along the transverse direction can be calculated as:

$$E_\mathrm{trans} = V_\mathrm{polymer} \cdot E_\mathrm{polymer} + V_\mathrm{fiber} \cdot E_\mathrm{fiber}$$

where $V_\mathrm{polymer}$ and $V_\mathrm{fiber}$ are the volume fractions of the polymer and the fiber, respectively. Since the volume fractions must add up to 1, we can write:

$$V_\mathrm{polymer} + V_\mathrm{fiber} = 1$$

Substituting $V_\mathrm{fiber} = 0.4$ and $E_\mathrm{polymer} = 3.5$ GPa, we can solve for $V_\mathrm{polymer}$:

$$V_\mathrm{polymer} + 0.4 = 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad V_\mathrm{polymer} = 0.6$$

Now we can calculate the elastic modulus of the composite material along the transverse direction:

$$E_\mathrm{trans} = 0.6 \cdot 3.5 + 0.4 \cdot 70 = 23.6 \ \mathrm{GPa}$$
