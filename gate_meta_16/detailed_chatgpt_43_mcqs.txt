We can use the expression for the critical nucleus size ($r^*$) in terms of the various parameters involved:

$r^* = \frac{2\gamma_{sl}}{\Delta G_{fusion}}\left(\frac{V_m}{k_BT}\ln{\frac{C_s}{C_l}}\right)^{1/3}$

where $\gamma_{sl}$ is the solid-liquid interfacial energy, $\Delta G_{fusion}$ is the Gibbs free energy of fusion, $V_m$ is the molar volume, $k_B$ is the Boltzmann constant, $T$ is the temperature, $C_s$ is the concentration of the solid in the equilibrium state, and $C_l$ is the concentration of the liquid in the same state.

To use this expression, we need to look up or calculate the values of $\Delta G_{fusion}$ and $C_s/C_l$. We can use the following relationships:

$\Delta G_{fusion} = -\Delta H_{fusion} = -N_A\cdot L_f$

$C_s/C_l = e^{-\Delta H_{fusion}/RT}$

where $\Delta H_{fusion}$ is the latent heat of fusion, $L_f$ is the molar latent heat of freezing (equal to $\Delta H_{fusion}$/mol), $N_A$ is Avogadro's number, and $R$ is the gas constant.

Using the given data, we get:

$\Delta H_{fusion} = 13000$ J/mol

$L_f = \frac{\Delta H_{fusion}}{N_A} = 2.16\times 10^8$ J/mol

$C_s/C_l = e^{-\Delta H_{fusion}/RT} = e^{-\frac{13000}{8.31\times 100}} = 0.905$

We can now substitute these values along with the others given in the expression for $r^*$:

$r^* = \frac{2\cdot 0.5}{2.16\times 10^8}\left(\frac{7\times 10^{-6}}{1.38\times 10^{-23}\cdot 1256}\ln{\frac{0.095}{1}}\right)^{1/3} = 1.55$ nm
