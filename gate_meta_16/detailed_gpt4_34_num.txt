To find the thermal contact resistance between the refractory and steel, we'll first find the temperature drop across the refractory and steel layers and later use the heat flux to determine the thermal contact resistance.

Given data:

Thermal conductivity of refractory, $k_1$ = 1.2 $W.m^{-1}K^{-1}$
Thickness of refractory lining, $d_1$ = 80 mm = 0.08 m
Thermal conductivity of steel, $k_2$ = 32 $W.m^{-1}K^{-1}$
Thickness of steel plate, $d_2$ = 4 mm = 0.004 m
Temperature at the surface of the refractory, $T_1$ = 1273 K
Temperature at the outer steel surface, $T_3$ = 473 K
Heat flux, $q$ = 1600 $W.m^{-2}$

Let $T_2$ be the temperature at the interface of the refractory-steel plate.

We'll use Fourier's law of heat conduction for the refractory layer and the steel plate layer.

Refractory layer:

$q = -k_1\frac{dT}{dx}$  (since heat flux is constant along the x-direction)

$-k_1\frac{T_2 - T_1}{d_1} = q$

Now, solve for $T_2$:

$T_2 = T_1 + \frac{q*d_1}{k_1}$
$T_2 = 1273 + \frac{1600*0.08}{1.2}$
$T_2 = 1273 + \frac{128}{1.2}$
$T_2 = 1273 + 106.67$
$T_2 ≈ 1379.67 K$

Next, we'll find the temperature drop across the steel plate layer, $ΔT_2$.

Steel plate layer:

$q = -k_2\frac{dT}{dx}$

$-k_2\frac{T_3 - T_2}{d_2} = q$

Now, solve for $ΔT_2$:

$ΔT_2 = T_2 - T_3 = \frac{q*d_2}{k_2}$
$ΔT_2 = 1379.67 - 473 = \frac{1600*0.004}{32}$
$ΔT_2 = 906.67 = \frac{64}{32}$
$ΔT_2 = 906.67 - 2$
$ΔT_2 ≈ 904.67 K$

Now we can calculate the thermal contact resistance, $R_{contact}$, between the refractory and steel layers:

$q = R_{contact} * ΔT_{contact}$

Where, $ΔT_{contact}$ = $ΔT_1 + ΔT_2 - ΔT_2 = 106.67 + 904.67 - 2$

So, the thermal contact resistance is:

$R_{contact} = \frac{q}{ΔT_{contact}}$
$R_{contact} = \frac{1600}{1009.34 - 2}$
$R_{contact} = \frac{1600}{1007.34}$
$R_{contact} ≈ 1.588 W^{-1}.m^2.K$

Thus, the thermal contact resistance between the refractory and steel layers is approximately 1.588 $W^{-1}.m^2.K$.

Answer: [1.588]
