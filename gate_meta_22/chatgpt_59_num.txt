Heat input($HI_{1}$) = ($I_{1}$*$V_{1}$*Efficiency*$S_{1}$)
where,
$I_{1}$=welding current of the first weld=200 A,
$V_{1}$=arc voltage of the first weld=18 V,
$Efficiency$=arc heat transfer efficiency=0.65,
$S_{1}$=welding speed of the first weld=0.002 $m.s^(-1)$

$HI_{1}$= (200*18*0.65*0.002)=$14.76 J/mm$

Also, $HI_{1}$=$HI_{2}$ (Both welds have identical heat input)

$HI_{2}$= ($I_{2}$*$V_{1}$*Efficiency*$S_{2}$)

where,
$I_{2}$=welding current of the second weld,
$S_{2}$=welding speed of the second weld=0.0022 $m.s^(-1)$

Substituting the values, we get:

($I_{2}$*18*0.65*0.0022)=$14.76 J/mm$

Solving for $I_{2}$, we get:

$I_{2}$=$\frac{14.76}{0.065*0.0022*18}$=487.40

Rounding off to the nearest integer, we get the welding current(in amperes) of the second weld as:

Answer: 487 A
