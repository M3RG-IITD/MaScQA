The strain rate sensitivity parameter is given by the following equation:

$$m=\frac{d\ln \sigma}{d\ln \dot{\epsilon}}$$

where $\sigma$ is the flow stress, and $\dot{\epsilon}$ is the strain rate.

We can find $m$ by taking the logarithmic derivative of the flow stress with respect to the strain rate:

$$\begin{aligned} m &= \frac{d\ln \sigma}{d\ln \dot{\epsilon}} \\ &= \frac{\ln \left(\frac{70 \text{ MPa}}{50 \text{ MPa}}\right)}{\ln\left(\frac{10 \text{ s}^{-1}}{0.1 \text{ s}^{-1}}\right)} \\ &= \frac{\ln (1.4)}{\ln 100} \approx \boxed{0.277} \end{aligned}$$

Therefore, the strain rate sensitivity parameter $m$ is approximately 0.277 (rounded off to 3 decimal places).
