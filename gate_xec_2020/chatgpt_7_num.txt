We can use the formula for maximum tolerable crack length in a brittle material as:

$a = [\frac{K_{IC}^2}{\sigma^2}(\frac{1}{\pi}(\frac{3f}{4})^2)]^{1/4}$

where,

$a =$ maximum tolerable crack length

$K_{IC} =$ fracture toughness

$\sigma =$ maximum stress

$f =$ geometrical factor (assumed to be 1.2 in this case)

Substituting the given values, we get:

$a = [\frac{(1 \text{ MPa}.m^{1/2})^2}{(200 \text{ MPa})^2}(\frac{1}{\pi}(\frac{3(1.2)}{4})^2)]^{1/4}$

Simplifying this expression gives:

$a = 30.63 \mu m$

Therefore, the maximum tolerable crack length is 30.63 micrometers.
