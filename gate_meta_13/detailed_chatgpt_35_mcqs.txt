The saturation magnetization is given by, 

$M_s = \frac{N\mu^2}{3k_BT}$

where, 
N = number of atoms per unit volume (in m^-3)
$\mu$ = magnetic moment per atom (in J/T),
$k_B$ = Boltzmann constant, and
T = temperature (in K).

Rearranging the above equation, we can find the magnetic moment per atom as:

$\mu = \sqrt{\frac{3k_BT M_s}{N}}$

For FCC crystal structure, the number of atoms per unit cell is 4. The volume of the unit cell (V) is given by:

$V = a^3/4$

where, a is the lattice parameter.

So, the number of atoms per unit volume is:

$N = 4/a^3$

Substituting the given values, 

$a=0.2$ nm = $2\times 10^{-10}$ m,

$M_s = 600\times 10^3$ A/m, and

using $k_B = 1.38 \times 10^{-23}$ J/K (Boltzmann Constant),

we get:

$T \mu^2 = \frac{M_s N}{3k_B} = \frac{600\times 10^3 \times 4 \times (\pi/6) \times (2\times 10^{-10})^3}{3 \times 1.38 \times 10^{-23}}$

Simplifying, we get:

$T\mu^2 \approx 8.82 \times 10^{-5}$

We need to find the value of $\mu$ in Bohr magneton (which is the unit of magnetic moment commonly used in atomic physics). 1 Bohr magneton ($\mu_B$) = $9.274 \times 10^{-24}$ J/T.

So, 

$\mu = \sqrt{\frac{T\mu^2}{T}} = \sqrt{\mu^2} = \sqrt{8.82 \times 10^{-5} / 1.38 \times 10^{-23}} \approx 2.02 \times 10^{-23} J/T$

$\mu$ in Bohr magneton = $\mu / \mu_B \approx 0.217$

Therefore, the correct answer is option (C) 0.517 (rounded off to three significant figures).
