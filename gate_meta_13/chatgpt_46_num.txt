We can use the equation $K_I = \sigma\sqrt{\pi a}$ to find the critical internal crack length $a$. Rearranging the equation, we get:

$a = \frac{K_I^2}{\sigma^2 \pi}$

Plugging in the values we have:

$a = \frac{(45\text{ MPa}\sqrt{\text{m}})^2}{(400\text{ MPa})^2 \pi}$

Simplifying this gives:

$a \approx 0.0016\text{ m} = \boxed{1.6\text{ mm}}$

Therefore, the critical internal crack length in the steel is 1.6 mm.
