From the given differential of Gibbs free energy, we can write:

$$dG = -SdT + VdP + \sum_{i=1}^{K} \mu_i dn_i$$

Taking partial derivative of $-SdT+VdP$ with respect to $T$ and $P$ respectively, we get:

$$\left(\frac{\partial (-S)}{\partial P}\right)_T = \left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial T}\right)_P$$

$$\left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial T}\right)_P = -\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial P}\right)_T $$

Thus, the correct option is:

(D) $\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial P}\right)_T = \left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial P}\right)_T$
