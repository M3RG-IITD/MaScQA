To find the room temperature electrical conductivity (σ), we can use the formula:

σ = q * (n * μ_e + p * μ_h)

where
q = charge of an electron = 1.6 * 10^(-19) C
n = electron concentration = 1 * 10^16 m^(-3)
p = hole concentration = 1 * 10^16 m^(-3)
μ_e = electron mobility = 0.1 m^2.V^(-1).s^(-1)
μ_h = hole mobility = 0.2 m^2.V^(-1).s^(-1)

Plugging the values into the formula:

σ = (1.6 * 10^(-19)) * ((1 * 10^16) * 0.1 + (1 * 10^16) * 0.2)
σ = (1.6 * 10^(-19)) * (0.3 * 10^16)
σ = 4.8 * 10^(-4) (Ω.m)^(-1)

So, the room temperature electrical conductivity is (D) 4.8 * 10^(-4) (Ω.m)^(-1).
