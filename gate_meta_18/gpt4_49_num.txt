First, let's find the volume $V$, surface area $A$, and mass $m$ of the droplet.

- Diameter $d = 1$ mm $= 1 \times 10^{-3}$ m
- Radius $r = \frac{d}{2} = 0.5 \times 10^{-3}$ m
- Volume $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi (0.5 \times 10^{-3})^3$ m$^3$
- Surface area $A = 4\pi r^2 = 4\pi (0.5 \times 10^{-3})^2$ m$^2$
- Mass $m = \rho V = 2700\times \frac{4}{3}\pi (0.5 \times 10^{-3})^3$ kg

Next, we'll use the gas-droplet convective heat transfer coefficient to determine the rate of heat transfer.

- Heat transfer rate $q = hA \Delta T = 200 \times 4\pi (0.5 \times 10^{-3})^2 \times (900 - 300)$ W

Now we'll find how much heat $Q$ must be removed to solidify the droplet.

- Heat of fusion $L_f = 4000$ kJ $kg^(-1) = 4000 \times 10^3$ J $kg^(-1)$
- Heat $Q = mL_f$

Finally, we'll find the time $t$ it takes to transfer this much heat.

- $t = \frac{Q}{q}$
