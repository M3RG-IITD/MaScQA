Given:

Enthalpy of fusion, $H_f = 4000 \,\text{kJ} \, kg^{-1} = 4 * 10^6 \,\text{J} \, kg^{-1}$

Heat transfer coefficient, $h = 200 \, \text{W} \, m^{-2} \text{K}^{-1}$

Density of liquid metal, $\rho = 2700 \,\text{kg} \, m^{-3}$

Droplet diameter, $d = 1 \,\text{mm} = 1 * 10^{-3} \,\text{m}$

Melting point of metal, $T_m = 900 \,\text{K}$

Temperature of gas stream, $T_g = 300 \,\text{K}$

We will assume that the entire droplet solidifies, and the solidification occurs uniformly from the surface to the center of the sphere. The energy transferred during the solidification process can be determined by the enthalpy change and the total volume of the droplet.

1. First, we need to find the volume of the droplet:

$$V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3$$

Where $r$ is the radius of the droplet. In our case, $r = \frac{d}{2} = 0.5 * 10^{-3} \,\text{m}$

$$V = \frac{4}{3} \pi (0.5 * 10^{-3})^3 = \frac{4}{3} \pi (1.25 * 10^{-10}) = 5.24 * 10^{-10} \,\text{m}^3$$

2. Now, we find the mass of the droplet using its density $\rho$:

$$m = \rho V$$

$$m = 2700 \cdot 5.24 * 10^{-10} = 1.4148 * 10^{-6} \,\text{kg}$$

3. Calculate the total energy required for solidification using the enthalpy of fusion:

$$Q = m \cdot H_f$$

$$Q = 1.4148 * 10^{-6} \cdot 4 * 10^6 = 5.6592 \,\text{J}$$

4. Determine the heat transfer rate, which is given by:

$$q = hA(T_m - T_g)$$

Where $A$ is the surface area of the droplet:

$$A = 4 \pi r^2$$

$$A = 4 \pi (0.5 * 10^{-3})^2 = 4 \pi (2.5 * 10^{-7}) = 3.14 * 10^{-6} \,m^2$$

Now substituting the given values, we have:

$$q = 200 \cdot 3.14 * 10^{-6} \cdot (900 - 300) = 200 \cdot 3.14 * 10^{-6} \cdot 600 = 0.3768 \,\text{W}$$

5. Finally, to find the time taken to complete the solidification process, we will use the formula:

$$t = \frac{Q}{q}$$

$$t = \frac{5.6592}{0.3768} = 15.0 \,\text{seconds (1 decimal place)}$$

The time taken to complete the solidification of a spherical droplet of diameter $1\,\text{mm}$ is $15.0$ seconds to one decimal place.

Hence, the answer is:

$$\boxed{[15.0]}$$
