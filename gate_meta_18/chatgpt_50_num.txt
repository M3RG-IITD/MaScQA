Given: 

Temperature (T) = 710 K

Vapour pressure of pure liquid Zn at 710 K, $p_{Zn}(X_{Zn}=1.0) = 3.6*10^{-4}$ atm

ln($\gamma_{Zn}$) = 0.875 * $(1-X_{Zn})^2$



We need to find the ratio of Zn vapour pressures at $X_{Zn}=0.7$ and $X_{Zn} = 1.0$. 

$p_{Zn}(X_{Zn}=0.7)$ can be calculated using Raoult's law: 

$p_{Zn}(X_{Zn}=0.7) = X_{Zn}*p_{Zn}(X_{Zn}=1.0) = 0.7 * 3.6*10^{-4} = 2.52*10^{-4}$ atm

Now, we can use this value and the given activity coefficient equation to calculate $p_{Zn}(X_{Zn}=0.7)/p_{Zn}(X_{Zn}=1.0)$:

ln($\gamma_{Zn}$) = 0.875 * $(1-X_{Zn})^2$

At $X_{Zn}=0.7$, 

ln($\gamma_{Zn}$) = 0.875 * $(1-0.7)^2 = 0.1225$

$\gamma_{Zn} = e^{0.1225} = 1.130$

$p_{Zn}(X_{Zn}=0.7)/p_{Zn}(X_{Zn}=1.0) = \gamma_{Zn}*X_{Zn}=1.130*0.7 = 0.791$

Therefore, the ratio of Zn vapour pressures at $X_{Zn}=0.7$ and $X_{Zn}=1.0$ is 0.79 (approx.) 

Answer: [0.79]
