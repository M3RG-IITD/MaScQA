Using the formula for the number of oxygen vacancies per mole in $CeO_2$:

$n = e^{-(\Delta H/k_BT)}\times N_A$

where:

$\Delta H$ is the enthalpy required to create an oxygen vacancy in $CeO_2$ = 4 eV

$k_B$ is Boltzmann’s constant = 8.62 * $10^(-5)$ eV/K

$T$ is the temperature = 1000 K

$N_A$ is Avogadro’s number = 6.02 * $10^(23)$ $mole^(-1)$

Substituting the given values:

$n = e^{-(4/(8.62*10^(-5) * 1000))}\times 6.02*10^(23)$

$n = 3.64\times 10^7$ vacancies/mole

Rounding off to the nearest integer,

$n$ = 36400000 vacancies/mole (rounded off to 36,400,000 vacancies/mole)
