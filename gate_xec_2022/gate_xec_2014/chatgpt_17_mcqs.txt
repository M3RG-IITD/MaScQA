$\Delta E = E_2 - E_4 = -\frac{13.6\mathrm{\ eV}}{2^2} + \frac{13.6\mathrm{\ eV}}{4^2} = 1.51\mathrm{\ eV}$

We can convert this to wavelength using the formula:

$\Delta E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$

where $h$ is Planck's constant, $c$ is the speed of light, and $\lambda$ is the wavelength.

Rearranging, we get:

$\lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E}$

$\lambda = \frac{(6.626\times10^{-34}\mathrm{\ J\ s})(2.998\times10^8\mathrm{\ m/s})}{(1.51\mathrm{\ eV})(1.602\times10^{-19}\mathrm{\ J/eV})} = 0.365\mathrm{\ Î¼m}$
