The zero point energy is given by the formula:

$E_0 = \frac{h^2}{8mL^2}$

where h is Planck's constant, m is the mass of the electron, and L is the width of the box.

First, we need to convert the width of the box from nm to m:

$L = 0.2\text{ nm} = 2\times10^{-10}\text{ m}$

Next, we can plug in the given values for h and m:

$E_0 = \frac{(6.63\times10^{-34}\text{ J s})^2}{8\times(9.11\times10^{-31}\text{ kg})\times(2\times10^{-10}\text{ m})^2}$

Simplifying the expression inside the brackets gives:

$(6.63\times10^{-34}\text{ J s})^2 = 4.39\times10^{-67}\text{ J}^2\text{s}^2$

$(2\times10^{-10}\text{ m})^2 = 4\times10^{-20}\text{ m}^2$

Substituting these values gives:

$E_0 = \frac{4.39\times10^{-67}\text{ J}^2\text{s}^2}{8\times(9.11\times10^{-31}\text{ kg})\times4\times10^{-20}\text{ m}^2}$

$E_0 = \frac{6.03\times10^{-27}\text{ J}}{(3.64\times10^{-29}\text{ kg/m})}$

$E_0 = 1.66\times10^{-2}\text{ J}$ (rounded off to 2 decimal places)

Finally, we convert the energy from joules to electronvolts:

$E_0 = \frac{1.66\times10^{-2}\text{ J}}{1.6\times10^{-19}\text{ J/ev}}$

$E_0 = 1.04\times10^{-17}\text{ ev}$

Rounding off to 1 decimal place gives the final answer:

$E_0 = \boxed{0.1\text{ ev}}$
