Step 1: Compute the volume of the bcc unit cell.
Step 2: Calculate the number of Fe atoms (Iron) in the bcc unit cell.

Step 4: Calculate the Bohr magneton (μB) using the given magnetic moment of Fe atoms.
Step 5: Calculate the saturation magnetization (Msat) by multiplying the number of Fe atoms per unit volume and the Bohr magneton (μB).

The lattice parameter (a) is given as 2.866 Å. We need to convert Å to meters: 1 Å = 1 × 10^(-10) m.

So, lattice parameter (a) in meters = 2.866 * 10^(-10) m.

The volume of the bcc unit cell (V) can be calculated using the formula:

V = a^3

V = (2.866 * 10^(-10))^3 = 2.353 * 10^(-29) m^3

In the bcc structure, there are 2 atoms per unit cell.

Step 3: Calculate the total number of Fe atoms per unit volume (n).

n = (Number of Fe atoms in bcc unit cell) / (Volume of bcc unit cell)

n = 2 / (2.353 * 10^(-29)) = 8.5 * 10^(28) atoms/m^3

Bohr magneton (μB) = 9.27 * 10^(-24) Am^2 (per Fe atom)

Step 5: Calculate the saturation magnetization (Msat) by multiplying the number of Fe atoms per unit volume and the Bohr magneton (μB).

Msat = (Number of Fe atoms per unit volume) * (Bohr magneton per Fe atom)

Msat = (8.5 * 10^(28) atoms/m^3) * (9.27 * 10^(-24) Am^2/atom)
