We can use the fracture toughness equation to determine the maximum crack length 'a':

$K_{IC} = f \cdot σ \cdot \sqrt{πa}$

We are given the fracture toughness, maximum stress, and the geometrical factor, so we'll plug those values into the equation and solve for 'a':

$1 MPa m^{1/2} = 1.2 \cdot 200 MPa \cdot \sqrt{πa}$

First, we'll isolate 'a' on one side of the equation:

$\sqrt{πa} = \frac{1 MPa m^{1/2}}{1.2 \cdot 200 MPa}$

Now we'll square both sides of the equation to cancel out the square root:

$πa = \left(\frac{1 MPa m^{1/2}}{1.2 \cdot 200 MPa}\right)^2$

Continuing to solve for 'a':

$a = \frac{1}{π} \left(\frac{1 MPa m^{1/2}}{1.2 \cdot 200 MPa}\right)^2$

Plug in the values and use a calculator to find 'a':

$a = \frac{1}{π} \left(\frac{1 \cancel{MPa} m^{1/2}}{1.2 \cdot 200 \cancel{MPa}}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{π}\left(\frac{1}{240}m^{1/2}\right)^2$

$a = \frac{1}{57600π} m \cdot \frac{1,000,000 \mu m}{1 m} = \frac{1,000,000}{57600π} \mu m ≈ 5.5 \mu m$
