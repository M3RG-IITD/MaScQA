We are given the diffusivity of A in the solid:

$D_A = (D_A)^° * exp(-\frac{E_a}{k_BT})$

Where:
$(D_A)^°$ is the diffusivity coefficient,
$E_a = 0.3$ eV is the activation energy,
$k_B = 8.62 * 10^{-5}$ eV/K is the Boltzmann's constant,
$T$ is the absolute temperature in Kelvin.

We are also given that at 300 K, the depth reached by component A in 1 hour is 10 μm.

We can treat diffusion in one dimension using Fick's Second law of diffusion:

$x = \sqrt{2 * D_A * t}$

Where $x$ is the depth, and $t$ is the time. 

At 300 K, we have the following known values:
$x_1 = 10 * 10^{-6}$ m (converting from μm to m)
$T_1 = 300$ K
$t_1 = 1$ hour $= 3600$ seconds (converting hours to seconds)

We should first find the diffusivity $D_A$ at 300 K.

$D_{A_1} = (D_A)^° * exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 300})$

Now, we can plug this value of $D_{A_1}$ into Fick's law:

$x_1 = \sqrt{2 * D_{A_1} * t_1}$

Now, we need to find the time $t_2$ for the same depth at 600 K. We first find the diffusivity at 600 K.

$D_{A_2} = (D_A)^° * exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 600})$

Using Fick's law again for $T_2 = 600$ K:

$x_1 = \sqrt{2 * D_{A_2} * t_2}$

We want to find $t_2$. We can first find the ratio between the diffusivities at the two temperatures:

$\frac{D_{A_2}}{D_{A_1}} = \frac{(D_A)^° * exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 600})}{(D_A)^° * exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 300})}$

The $(D_A)^°$ terms cancel out:

$\frac{D_{A_2}}{D_{A_1}} = \frac{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 600})}{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 300})}$

Now, we can use the ratio of diffusivities to find the ratio of times:

$\frac{t_2}{t_1} = \frac{D_{A_1}}{D_{A_2}} = \frac{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 300})}{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 600})}$

We already know $t_1 = 3600$ seconds:

$t_2 = t_1 * \frac{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 300})}{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 600})}$ 

$t_2 = 3600 * \frac{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 300})}{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 600})}$

Now, we can calculate the value of $t_2$:

$t_2 = 3600 * \frac{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 300})}{exp(-\frac{0.3}{8.62 * 10^{-5} * 600})} \approx 667.2$ seconds

Rounded off to 1 decimal place, the time taken for A to diffuse to the same depth at 600 K is 667.2 seconds.
