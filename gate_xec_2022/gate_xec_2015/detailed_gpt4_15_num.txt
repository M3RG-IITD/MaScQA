To calculate the capacitance of this capacitor, we will use the formula:

$$
C = \varepsilon_0 \varepsilon_r \frac{A}{d}
$$

where:
- $C$ is the capacitance of the capacitor
- $\varepsilon_0$ is the vacuum permittivity (8.854 x 10^(-12) F/m)
- $\varepsilon_r$ is the relative permittivity (dielectric constant) of the material
- $A$ is the electrode area
- $d$ is the thickness of the dielectric

Given values are:

- Dielectric constant ($\varepsilon_r$) = 2000
- Dielectric thickness (d) = 0.075 cm
- Electrode area (A) = 0.2 $cm^2$

We need to convert the dielectric thickness and electrode area into meters before using them in the formula. 

1 cm = 0.01 m

Dielectric thickness (d) = 0.075 cm * 0.01 m/cm = 0.00075 m
Electrode area (A) = 0.2 $cm^2$ * (0.01 m/cm)^2 = 2 x 10^(-5) $m^2$

Now we can calculate the capacitance (C):

$$
C = \varepsilon_0 \varepsilon_r \frac{A}{d} = (8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{F/m}) \times 2000 \times \frac{2 \times 10^{-5} \text{m}^2}{0.00075 \text{m}}
$$

$$
C = 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{F/m} \times 2000 \times \frac{2 \times 10^{-5} \text{m}^2}{0.00075 \text{m}} \approx 4.71 \times 10^{-10} \text{F}
$$

Now, let's convert the capacitance from farads (F) to nanofarads (nF):

$$
1 \text{ F} = 10^9 \text{ nF}
$$

$$
C = 4.71 \times 10^{-10} \text{F} \times \frac{10^9 \text{nF}}{1 \text{F}} = 471 \text{ nF}
$$

The capacitance of this capacitor is approximately 471 nF.

The correct answer is [471].
