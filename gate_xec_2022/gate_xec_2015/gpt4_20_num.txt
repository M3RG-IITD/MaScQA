We can use the Arrhenius equation to relate the diffusion coefficient (D) and temperature (T):

$D(T) = D_0 e^{(-Q/RT)}$

Where:
- D(T) is the diffusion coefficient at temperature T,
- D_0 is the pre-exponential factor,
- Q is the activation energy,
- R is the gas constant (8.314 J/mol K),
- T is the temperature in Kelvin.

We are given D(T) at two different temperatures, T1 = 400K and T2 = 500K:

$D_1 = 1.28 \times 10^{-22} m^2s^{-1}$
$D_2 = 5.75 \times 10^{-19} m^2s^{-1}$

We can form two equations:

1) $D_1 = D_0 e^{(-Q/RT_1)} = 1.28 \times 10^{-22}$
2) $D_2 = D_0 e^{(-Q/RT_2)} = 5.75 \times 10^{-19}$

Divide equation 2 by equation 1:

$\frac{D_2}{D_1} = \frac{e^{-Q/RT_2}}{e^{-Q/RT_1}}$

Simplify the equation:

$\frac{5.75 \times 10^{-19}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}} = e^{Q/R(1/T_1 - 1/T_2)}$

Solve the equation for Q:
$\ln{\frac{5.75 \times 10^{-19}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}} = \frac{Q}{R} \left(\frac{1}{400} - \frac{1}{500}\right)$

$Q = R \ln{\frac{5.75 \times 10^{-19}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}} \left(\frac{1}{400} - \frac{1}{500}\right)^{-1}$

Now, we are asked to find the temperature (T) when:

$D_3 = 10^{-16} m^2s^{-1}$

Use the Arrhenius equation again:

$10^{-16} = D_0 e^{-Q/RT}$

But now we have the expression for Q from above. We can substitute it into the current equation:

$10^{-16} = D_0 e^{-(R \ln{\frac{5.75 \times 10^{-19}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}} \left(\frac{1}{400} - \frac{1}{500}\right)^{-1}) / (RT)}$

To solve for T, we can divide this equation by equation 1 from above:

$\frac{10^{-16}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}} = \frac{e^{-(R \ln{\frac{5.75 \times 10^{-19}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}} \left(\frac{1}{400} - \frac{1}{500}\right)^{-1}) / (RT)}}{e^{(-Q/RT_1)}}$

And simplify:

$\frac{10^{-16}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}} = e^{Q/R(1/T_1 - 1/T)}$

Repeating the process as before, we solve for T (let's call it T3):

$\ln{\frac{10^{-16}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}} = \frac{Q}{R} \left(\frac{1}{400} - \frac{1}{T_3}\right)$

$T_3 = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{400} - (\frac{R}{Q}\ln{\frac{10^{-16}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}})}$

Now substitute the expression for Q from above:

$T_3 = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{400} - \frac{\ln{\frac{10^{-16}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}}}{\ln{\frac{5.75 \times 10^{-19}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}} \left(\frac{1}{400} - \frac{1}{500}\right)}}$

Plug in the values and solve for T_3 (use a calculator if necessary).

$T_3 = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{400} - \frac{\ln{\frac{10^{-16}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}}}{\ln{\frac{5.75 \times 10^{-19}}{1.28 \times 10^{-22}}} \left(\frac{1}{400} - \frac{1}{500}\right)}} \approx 531.27K$

So the temperature at which the diffusion coefficient is $10^{-16} m^2s^{-1}$ is approximately 531.27 Kelvin.

The answer is [531.27].
