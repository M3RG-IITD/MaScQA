To calculate the change in enthalpy, we need to integrate the expression for $C_p$ with respect to temperature from 25°C to 700°C, and then multiply by the number of moles of iron. Note that the units of $C_p$ are J/(mol.K), so we need to convert the temperature from °C to K:

25°C = 298 K
700°C = 973 K

Integrating $C_p$ with respect to T gives:

$$\Delta H =\int_{298}^{973} C_p \mathrm{d}T = \int_{298}^{973} (17.49 + 24.77\times 10^{-3} T)\mathrm{d}T $$

$$\Delta H = \left[ 17.49T + 12.39 \times 10^{-3}T^2 \right]_{298}^{973} $$

$$\Delta H = [(17.49\times 973 + 12.39 \times 10^{-3}\times 973^2) - (17.49\times 298 + 12.39 \times 10^{-3}\times 298^2)]\mathrm{J/mol} $$

$$\Delta H = 6.13 \times 10^4 \mathrm{J/mol}$$

Therefore, the change in enthalpy of pure iron when it is heated from 25°C to 700°C is 6.13 × 10^4 J/mol.
