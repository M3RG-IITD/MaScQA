Using Chvorinov's equation:

$T_V = C_{Chv} \times V^{n+1}$

where $T_V$ is the time for solidification of the casting or riser, $C_{Chv}$ is the Chvorinov constant, $V$ is the volume and $n$ is the exponent.

For the cubic casting:

$T_{Vc} = C_{Chv} \times V_c^{n+1}$

where $V_c = 5.0 \text{ cm} \times 5.0 \text{ cm} \times 5.0 \text{ cm} = 125 \text{ cm}^3$ is the volume of the casting.

For the cylindrical riser:

$T_{Vr} = C_{Chv} \times V_r^{n+1}$

where $V_r = \pi \times \left(\frac{d_r}{2}\right)^2 \times h_r$ is the volume of the riser, $d_r$ is the diameter and $h_r$ is the height.

Given that the ratio of diameter to height is 0.5, we can write:

$\frac{d_r}{h_r} = 0.5$

$d_r = 0.5h_r$

The diameter can also be expressed in terms of the volume as:

$V_r = \pi \times \left(\frac{d_r}{2}\right)^2 \times h_r = \frac{\pi h_r^3}{16}$

Substituting these expressions in the Chvorinov equation for the riser:

$T_{Vr} = C_{Chv} \times \left(\frac{\pi h_r^3}{16}\right)^{n+1}$

We are given that $n = 2$ and that $T_{Vc}T_{Vr} = (\text{total time})^2 = (1.6 \text{ min})^2 = 2.56 \text{ min}^2$

Also, $T_{Vr} = 3.2 \text{ min}$

Substituting these values, we can solve for $h_r$:

$T_{Vc}T_{Vr} = C_{Chv}^2 \times V_c^{n+1} \times \left(\frac{\pi h_r^3}{16}\right)^{n+1} = \left(C_{Chv} \times \frac{\pi}{16}\right)^{n+1} \times V_c^{n+1} \times h_r^{(n+1)(n+1)}$

$h_r = \left(\frac{T_{Vc}T_{Vr}}{\left(C_{Chv} \times \frac{\pi}{16}\right)^{n+1} \times V_c^{n+1}}\right)^{\frac{1}{(n+1)(n+1)}}$

Substituting known values:

$h_r = \left(\frac{(1.6 \text{ min})^2}{\left(C_{Chv} \times \frac{\pi}{16}\right)^3 \times (125 \text{ cm}^3)^3}\right)^{\frac{1}{9}}$

We do not have the value of the Chvorinov constant, so we cannot calculate the numerical value of $h_r$.
