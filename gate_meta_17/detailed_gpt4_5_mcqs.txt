To find the standard cell potential for the given electrochemical reaction, we need to use the Nernst equation, which is:

E°(cell) = E°(cathode) - E°(anode)

In this reaction, Cu⁺² will be reduced to Cu and Zn will be oxidized to Zn²⁺. So, Cu²⁺/Cu is the cathode and Zn²⁺/Zn is the anode.

Given, E°(Cu²⁺/Cu) = 0.337 V and E°(Zn²⁺/Zn) = -0.763 V

Now, using the Nernst equation:

E°(cell) = E°(Cu²⁺/Cu) - E°(Zn²⁺/Zn)
E°(cell) = 0.337 V - (-0.763 V)

Adding the values:

E°(cell) = 0.337 V + 0.763 V
E°(cell) = 1.100 V
