We can start by using the given information to calculate the volume of the unit cell:

$a = 0.4~\text{nm} = 0.4 \times 10^{-9}~\text{m}$ (lattice parameter)

The unit cell of a cubic metal has atoms only at the corners of a cube, so the number of atoms in the unit cell is 8.

Now, we can find the mass of the unit cell by multiplying the mass of one atom by the number of atoms in the unit cell:

$183~\text{g/mol} = 183 \times 10^{-3}~\text{kg/mol}$ (atomic weight)

$N_\text{A} = 6.022 \times 10^{23}~\text{mol}^{-1}$ (Avogadro's number)

$183~\text{g/mol} \div N_\text{A} = 3.031 \times 10^{-22}~\text{kg/atom}$

$8~\text{atoms} \times 3.031 \times 10^{-22}~\text{kg/atom} = 2.425 \times 10^{-21}~\text{kg}$ (mass of the unit cell)

Finally, we can use the density formula to find the volume of the unit cell:

$\rho = \frac{m}{V}$

$V = \frac{m}{\rho} = \frac{2.425 \times 10^{-21}~\text{kg}}{19000~\text{kg/m}^3} = 1.276 \times 10^{-25}~\text{m}^3$

Therefore, the effective number of atoms in the unit cell is:

$\frac{8~\text{atoms} \times (\frac{a}{2})^3}{V} = \frac{8 \times (0.4 \times 10^{-9}/2)^3}{1.276 \times 10^{-25}} = \boxed{4}$
