Using the Griffith's criterion, we can find the fracture strength (σ) of this brittle material with the given crack length (a), Young's modulus (E) and surface energy (γ).

Griffith's criterion is expressed as:

σ = (2 * γ * E) / (π * a)

We first convert the given values to SI units:

Young's modulus (E): 60 GPa = 60 * 10^9 Pa
Surface energy (γ): 0.5 J/m² = 0.5 N/m
Crack length (a): 2µm = 2 * 10^(-6)m

Substitute these values into the expression above:

σ = (2 * 0.5 N/m * 60 * 10^9 Pa) / (π * 2 * 10^(-6)m)

σ ≈ 1.897 * 10^10 Pa

Now, convert the fracture strength back to MPa:

Fracture strength (σ) ≈ 1.897 * 10^10 Pa * (1 MPa / 10^6 Pa) ≈ 18,970 MPa ≈ 18.97 GPa

The fracture strength of this brittle material is approximately 18.97 GPa.

Answer: [18.97]
