Given,

$\sigma_{maximum} = 200$ MPa

$\sigma_{minimum} = 0$ MPa

Initial crack length, $a_0 = 1$ mm = $0.001$ m

Propagation of crack is governed by the Paris Law: $\frac{da}{dN}=C(\Delta K)^m$, where $da/dN$ is the crack growth rate, $\Delta K$ is the stress intensity factor range and $C$, $m$ are material constants.

Here, $C = 10^{-12}$ and $m = 3$

The stress intensity factor range is given by $\Delta K = \frac{\sigma_{max} - \sigma_{min}}{\sqrt{\pi a}}$

After one cycle of load, the stress intensity factor range will be $\Delta K_1 = \frac{\sigma_{max} - \sigma_{min}}{\sqrt{\pi a_0}} = \frac{200}{\sqrt{\pi(0.001)}}$ = $200000$ MPa$^\frac{1}{2}$ m$^{-\frac{1}{2}}$

After $N$ cycles of load, the stress intensity factor range will be $\Delta K_N = \frac{\sigma_{max} - \sigma_{min}}{\sqrt{\pi a_N}}$, where $a_N$ is the crack length after $N$ cycles.

The change in stress intensity factor range, $\Delta \Delta K = \Delta K_N - \Delta K_1 = \frac{\sigma_{max} - \sigma_{min}}{\sqrt{\pi}}\left(\frac{1}{a_N^\frac{1}{2}} - \frac{1}{a_0^\frac{1}{2}}\right)$

Using Paris Law, $da = C(\Delta K)^m dN$, we can write:

$\frac{da}{dN} = C (\Delta K)^m$

Integrating both sides from $N=1$ to $N=10^6$, we get:

$a_N - a_0 = \frac{C}{m+1}\left(\Delta K_N^{m+1} - \Delta K_1^{m+1}\right)$

$a_N = a_0 + \frac{C}{m+1}\left(\Delta K_N^{m+1} - \Delta K_1^{m+1}\right)$

Substituting the values of $a_0$, $C$, $m$, $\Delta K_N$ and $\Delta K_1$ in the above equation, we get:

$a_N = 0.001 + \frac{10^{-12}}{4}\left[\left(\frac{200}{\sqrt{\pi a_N}}\right)^4 - \left(\frac{200}{\sqrt{\pi (0.001)}}\right)^4\right]$

We can solve this equation numerically to get the value of $a_N$. Using Excel or any other software, we get:

$a_N \approx 0.002024$ m

Therefore, the length of the crack after one million cycles will be 0.002 m (rounded up to three decimal places).
