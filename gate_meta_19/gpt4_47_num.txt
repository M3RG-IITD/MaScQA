We are given the following values:
Shear modulus of iron (G) = 82 GPa = 82 × 10^9 N/m²
Burger’s vector (b) = a/2[1 1 1]
Edge length (a) = 0.2856 nm = 0.2856 × 10^(-9) m

We can find the magnitude of b from b = a/2[1 1 1] using the formula b = a/(2*√3) = a/√2:

b = (0.2856 × 10^(-9)) m / √2 = (0.202 × 10^(-9)) m

The dislocation density increases from 10^10 to 10^15 m^(-2). The change in dislocation density (Δρ) is:

Δρ = (10^15 - 10^10) m^(-2) = 0.999 × 10^15 m^(-2)

The stored energy (E) per unit volume can be calculated using the formula:

E = (1/2) * G * b² * Δρ

Now, substituting the given values:

E = (1/2) * (82 × 10^9 N/m²) * ((0.202 × 10^(-9)) m)² * (0.999 × 10^15 m^(-2))

E = 0.5 * 82 * 10^9 * 0.202^2 * 10^(-18) * 0.999 * 10^15

E = 0.5 * 82 * 0.040804 * 0.999 * 10^(-3)

E = 1.35556 MJ/m³

Rounding off to one decimal place, we have:

E = 1.4 MJ/m³

So, the associated stored energy is [1.4] MJ/m³.
