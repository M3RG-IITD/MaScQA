The maximum stress that the plate can sustain can be calculated using the following equation:
$$\sigma_{max}=\dfrac{K_{1c}}{\sqrt{\pi a}}$$

Where:
- $K_{1c}$ is the fracture toughness
- $a$ is the crack length

Substituting the given values:
$$\sigma_{max}=\dfrac{20}{\sqrt{\pi \times \frac{80}{\pi}}}$$
$$\sigma_{max}=\dfrac{20}{\sqrt{80}}$$
$$\sigma_{max}=2.236 \text{ MPa}$$

Rounding off to the nearest integer, we get:
$$\boxed{\sigma_{max}=2 \text{ MPa}}$$
