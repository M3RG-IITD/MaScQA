We are given the fracture toughness $K_{1c}$ and the length of the crack $a = \frac{80}{\pi}$mm. We need to find the maximum applied stress $\sigma$ that the plate can sustain in mode I. 

Linear elastic fracture mechanics states that the stress intensity factor for a through-thickness crack in an infinite plate under mode I loading is given by:

$K_I = \sigma \sqrt{\pi a}$

The maximum applied stress can be determined by setting $K_I$ equal to the fracture toughness $K_{1c}$:

$K_{1c} = \sigma \sqrt{\pi a}$

Solving for the maximum applied stress $\sigma$, we get:

$\sigma = \frac{K_{1c}}{\sqrt{\pi a}}$

Now, plugging in the given values:

$\sigma = \frac{20 \text{ MPa}\ m^{1/2}}{\sqrt{\pi \times \frac{80}{\pi} \text{mm}}} = \frac{20 \text{ MPa}\ m^{1/2}}{\sqrt{80 \text{mm}}} = \frac{20 \times 10^6 \text{ N/m}^2}{\sqrt{80} \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}} \approx 2245016.012 \text{ N/m}^2$

Converting to MPa:

$\sigma \approx 2.245 \text{ MPa}$

Rounding off to the nearest integer, we get a maximum applied stress of 2 MPa. The answer is [2].
