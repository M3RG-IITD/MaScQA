The given velocity profile is $V_x = 0.5y - 0.5y^3$.

To determine the boundary layer thickness, we can use the equation:

$\frac{\delta}{x} = 5 \sqrt{\frac{\mu}{\rho V_{\infty}} \frac{dV_x}{dy} \bigg|_{y=0}}$

where $\mu$ is the viscosity, $\rho$ is the density and $x$ is the distance from the leading edge.

Taking the derivative of $V_x$ with respect to $y$:

$\frac{dV_x}{dy} = 0.5 - 1.5y^2$

Evaluating the derivative at $y=0$ gives:

$\frac{dV_x}{dy} \bigg|_{y=0} = 0.5$

Substituting the given values and solving:

$\frac{\delta}{x} = 5 \sqrt{\frac{\mu}{\rho V_{\infty}} \frac{dV_x}{dy} \bigg|_{y=0}} = 5 \sqrt{\frac{\mu}{\rho V_{\infty}} \cdot 0.5}$

The Prandtl number for water at room temperature is 7. So, using this value along with the given values of density and viscosity of water, we have:

$\frac{\delta}{x} = 5 \sqrt{\frac{(1.002 \cdot 10^{-3} kg/m.s)}{(1000 kg/m^3) (V_{\infty})} \cdot 0.5} = \frac{5 \cdot 0.0251}{\sqrt{V_{\infty}}}$

Rounding off to 2 decimal places:

$\frac{\delta}{x} = 0.13 \sqrt{\frac{V_{\infty}}{m/s}}$

Hence, the boundary layer thickness at $x=x_1$ is given by $\frac{\delta}{x} = 0.13 \sqrt{\frac{V_{\infty}}{m/s}}$.
