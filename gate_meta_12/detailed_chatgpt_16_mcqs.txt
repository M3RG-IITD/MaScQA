The inter-planar spacing of a plane is given by the formula:

$$d = \frac{a}{\sqrt{h^2+k^2+l^2}}$$

where $a$ is the lattice constant, and $h,k,l$ are the Miller indices of the plane.

For the planes {nh nk nl}, we have:

$$d' = \frac{a}{\sqrt{(nh)^2+(nk)^2+(nl)^2}}$$

Dividing the second equation by the first, we get:

$$\frac{d'}{d} = \frac{\frac{a}{\sqrt{(nh)^2+(nk)^2+(nl)^2}}}{\frac{a}{\sqrt{h^2+k^2+l^2}}}$$

Simplifying the expression, we get:

$$\frac{d'}{d} = \frac{\sqrt{h^2+k^2+l^2}}{\sqrt{(nh)^2+(nk)^2+(nl)^2}}$$

Using the identity $(ab)^2 = a^2 b^2$, we can write:

$$(nh)^2 = n^2 h^2$$

$$(nk)^2 = n^2 k^2$$

$$(nl)^2 = n^2 l^2$$

Substituting these values in the denominator, we get:

$$\frac{d'}{d} = \frac{\sqrt{h^2+k^2+l^2}}{n\sqrt{h^2+k^2+l^2}} = \frac{1}{n}$$

Therefore, the inter-planar spacing of the planes {nh nk nl} is:

$$d' = \frac{d}{n}$$

Hence, the answer is option (B) $d/n$.
